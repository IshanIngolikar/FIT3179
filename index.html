Australian Healthcare Data Visualization

Hospital Distribution Map

Distribution of hospitals across regions.

This map shows how healthcare establishments are concentrated primarily along the eastern coastline, where population densities are highest, the map reveals a disparity in healthcare availability, with sparse hospital coverage in the vast, less populated central and western regions.

This map reveals the variability in hospital bed availability per capita across Australian states and territories, highlighting the Northern Territory as having the highest density of hospital beds relative to its population.

This visualization prompts a critical examination of healthcare resource allocation, suggesting a potential surplus in less populous areas like the NT and possible shortages in densely populated regions such as New South Wales and Victoria.
